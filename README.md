## Employee Health Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee health data using Microsoft Excel to identify workplace health trends, assess employee risk factors, and provide insights to support workforce wellness and organizational decision-making.

## Project Objectives

- Explore employee health patterns.
- Identify workplace health risks.
- Assess vaccination coverage.
- Evaluate absenteeism trends.
- Support employee wellness planning through interactive reporting.


## Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel
- Power Query
- Pivot Tables
- Pivot Charts
- Slicers
- Conditional Formatting


## Data Cleaning & Preparation

- Cleaned and transformed data using Power Query.
- Standardized names and formatting.
- Removed duplicate records.
- Handled missing values.
- Corrected data inconsistencies.
- Created helper columns to support analysis.

## Key Insights

- Only 24% of employees are fully vaccinated, while 76% are either unvaccinated, partially vaccinated, or have unknown vaccination status, increasing the risk of infectious disease transmission.

- Although the Finance department recorded the highest total sick days due to its larger workforce, the HR and Legal departments recorded the highest average sick days per employee (approximately 11 days each in 2024), indicating worsening health outcomes or increased workload pressure.

- Medium-risk employees represent 64% of the workforce and were the only risk category to experience an increase in average sick days (from 10 to 11 days), suggesting a growing need for preventive interventions.

- While the Finance department recorded the highest overall health risk score, the Operations department had the highest average health risk score (3.00/5) and the lowest average mental health score (4.88/10) despite reporting very low sick leave. This pattern may indicate presenteeism, where employees continue working despite poor health.

- Hypertension was the most common chronic condition, affecting 22% of employees. Approximately 45% of employees reported no chronic condition; however, this group also recorded the highest average missed workdays, suggesting the influence of temporary illnesses or population size.


## Recommendations

- Increase vaccination uptake through workplace vaccination campaigns and employee education.
- Review workload and wellbeing initiatives for HR, Legal, and medium-risk employees to reduce absenteeism.
- Investigate potential presenteeism within the Operations department through employee wellbeing assessments.
- Expand hypertension screening, health education, and preventive wellness programmes.
- Improve health data collection and monitoring to better distinguish chronic health risks from short-term illnesses.


## Conclusion

The analysis highlights opportunities to strengthen employee health through improved vaccination coverage, targeted wellbeing interventions, chronic disease prevention, and enhanced workforce health monitoring. These findings can support evidence-based workplace health policies and employee wellness initiatives.
